## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 40-year-old male with a history of mild hypertension who presented to the emergency department complaining of a rapidly progressive, excruciatingly painful swelling on his right forearm for the past four days. The patient stated that the area began as a small, firm bump following an insect bite, which quickly grew in size, turned dark red, and became so tender that he was unable to move his wrist freely. On physical examination, he was mildly febrile to 38.0 degrees Celsius, with a blood pressure of 138/84 mmHg and a heart rate of 88 bpm. Right upper extremity inspection revealed a highly prominent, 4-centimeter indurated, fluctuant mass on the volar aspect of the mid-forearm, surrounded by a wide zone of warm, tense, erythematous cellulitis extending toward the antecubital fossa. Linear erythematous streaking was noted along the medial arm, though axillary lymphadenopathy was absent. Radial and ulnar pulses were strong, and distal capillary refill was immediate. Laboratorial screening was significant for a moderate leukocytosis and elevated inflammatory markers. A point-of-care ultrasound confirmed a mature subcutaneous abscess cavity, requiring immediate operative incision, drainage, and packing within the emergency unit.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe localized pain, an exquisitely tender, fluctuant 4cm indurated mass on the right volar forearm, tracking streaks of erythema, localized warmth, low-grade fever.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Acute Skin and Soft Tissue Abscess of the Right Forearm (Methicillin-Resistant Staphylococcus aureus), Severe Localized Cellulitis

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Surgical incision and drainage (I&D) performed in the emergency procedure room under local anesthesia, packing of the cavity with iodoform gauze, initiation of intravenous Vancomycin (avoiding Sulfa drugs), local wound care, and upper extremity elevation.
