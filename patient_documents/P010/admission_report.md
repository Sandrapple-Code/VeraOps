## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is an 81-year-old male with a baseline history of moderate Alzheimer's dementia and benign prostatic hyperplasia who was brought to the emergency department by his wife due to a sudden, profound decline in his mental status and a complete refusal to eat or drink over the preceding 36 hours. The wife noted that he had become uncharacteristically lethargic, sleeping for most of the day, was unable to recognize close family members, and exhibited new-onset urinary incontinence. In the emergency bay, the patient was obtunded, responding only briefly to loud verbal commands, and was mildly hypothermic with a core rectal temperature of 35.9 degrees Celsius. His blood pressure was stable at 112/64 mmHg, but he was tachycardic with a heart rate of 104 beats per minute. Physical examination was limited due to poor cooperation, but demonstrated mild grimacing upon deep suprapubic palpation and dry oral mucosa. Laboratory workup revealed a moderate leukocytosis and a urinalysis highly diagnostic of a urinary tract infection. Due to his advanced age, hypothermia, and altered sensorium, blood cultures were drawn immediately, and he was started empirically on intravenous Ceftriaxone. Within 24 hours, both initial blood culture bottles flagged positive for Gram-negative rods, subsequently identified as Escherichia coli, establishing a diagnosis of E. coli bacteremia secondary to acute pyelonephritis in the setting of chronic urinary retention from BPH. He was admitted to the general medicine geriatric service for continuous IV antibiotics, fluid management, and delirium monitoring.

## Initial Clinical Presentation

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
