## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 31-year-old male ultra-marathon athlete with no significant past medical history who presented to the emergency department complaining of an acute onset of excruciating, unremitting muscle pain, profound stiffness, and generalized weakness in his bilateral lower limbs that commenced a few hours after an intense, long-distance endurance run. The patient also noted with high anxiety that he had become unable to walk normally and had passed a small volume of dark, tea-colored urine. Upon physical examination, he was afebrile and normotensive, though visibly distressed and tachycardic at 102 bpm. Exquisite, diffuse tenderness to palpation and prominent firmness were noted across the muscle bellies of his bilateral quadriceps, hamstrings, and calves, with a slightly restricted active range of motion at the knees due to pain. There were no signs of compartment syndrome, and distal pulses were strong and equal. Initial laboratory screenings revealed a critical elevation in serum creatine kinase at 45,000 U/L and an active acute kidney injury secondary to myoglobin toxicity, requiring immediate admission to the emergency observation service for aggressive volume expansion.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe, diffuse, crushing muscle pain localized to the bilateral quadriceps and calves, profound generalized weakness, passing dark 'tea-colored' urine, mild nausea.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Severe Exertional Rhabdomyolysis, Acute Kidney Injury (Stage 1), Moderate Dehydration

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Aggressive intravenous fluid resuscitation utilizing Normal Saline at a high rate of 250-300 mL/hour to maintain an aggressive urine output goal of >200 mL/hour, preventing myoglobin cast precipitation within the renal tubules; serial tracking of serum electrolytes and creatine kinase levels every 6 hours; strict avoidance of all statin medications; bed rest.
