## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 32-year-old male defense attorney with a history of morbid obesity and neglected type 2 diabetes who was admitted to the general medicine service following an emergency room presentation for acute respiratory symptoms and high fevers. The patient reported a four-day history of a worsening, deep chest cough producing thick yellowish sputum, accompanied by a sharp, stabbing right-sided chest pain that intensified significantly during coughing fits or deep breathing, causing him to take shallow breaths. On physical examination, he was febrile to 39.1 degrees Celsius, tachypneic at 24 breaths per minute, and tachycardic at 104 bpm, with an oxygen saturation of 91% on room air. He was noted to be in moderate distress, with physical findings heavily impacted by his Class II obesity. Auscultation of the lungs revealed localized bronchial breath sounds and coarse crackles isolated to the right lower lung base, with normal air entry on the left. Laboratory testing was significant for a marked leukocytosis and severe hyperglycemia, with an HbA1c confirming sustained poor diabetic control. A chest radiograph demonstrated a clear, dense lobar consolidation within the right lower lobe. Given his high metabolic risk profile, poorly controlled diabetes, and early hypoxia, he was admitted for intravenous antibiotics and insulin optimization.
