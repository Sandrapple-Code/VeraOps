## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 37-year-old female former military pilot with a known history of rapidly progressive idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis who was admitted to the pulmonology service via the emergency department due to an acute, severe decline in her respiratory status over the preceding 48 hours. The patient reported a sudden, dramatic increase in her baseline shortness of breath, rendering her unable to speak or ambulate across a room, accompanied by an exhausting, non-productive dry cough. Upon physical examination, she was in profound respiratory distress, tachypleic at 32 breaths per minute, highly tachycardic with a heart rate of 124 breaths per minute, and maintaining an oxygen saturation of only 74% on room air, which corrected poorly to 88% on a standard 6L nasal cannula. Auscultation of the chest revealed classical, harsh, bilateral end-inspiratory 'Velcro' crackles audible throughout all lung zones, with zero wheezing. Broad laboratory screens ruled out an acute cardiac event or pulmonary embolism, but an arterial blood gas confirmed severe hypoxemia. A high-resolution chest CT scan confirmed advanced interstitial lung disease with widespread ground-glass changes, establishing a high-mortality diagnosis of an acute IPF exacerbation and necessitating immediate intensive respiratory admission.
