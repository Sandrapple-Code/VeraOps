## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 65-year-old male with a significant medical history of poorly controlled hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and coronary artery disease post-CABG who presented to the emergency department complaining of a sudden, severe, throbbing occipital headache that woke him from sleep, accompanied by marked dizziness, blurred vision, and a mild, familiar crushing sensation in his chest. Upon initial examination in the triage bay, the patient was noted to be extremely agitated and volatile. His initial blood pressure was critically elevated at 210/115 mmHg in both arms, satisfying the criteria for a hypertensive crisis. His heart rate was 92 breaths per minute, respiratory rate was 18 breaths per minute, and he was afebrile. Laboratory workup showed stable cardiac enzymes but mild acute kidney injury. The patient was immediately started on oral antihypertensive agents and admitted to the telemetry service for careful, step-wise blood pressure reduction to prevent cerebral hypoperfusion.
