## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 41-year-old male with an extensive background history of repetitive joint injuries and physical trauma who was admitted to the orthopedic surgical service for a planned, elective left shoulder arthroscopic rotator cuff repair. The patient had been suffering from chronic, worsening left shoulder pain for over two years, which severely restricted his overhead mobility, caused significant nighttime pain, and failed a six-month course of dedicated physical therapy and localized corticosteroid injections. Radiographic and MRI evaluations confirmed a large, full-thickness supraspinatus tendon tear requiring operative reconstruction. The patient was cleared from a cardiovascular standpoint for general anesthesia, with a specific high-priority alert placed in his chart to strictly avoid hydromorphone derivatives due to a history of severe respiratory sensitivity.
