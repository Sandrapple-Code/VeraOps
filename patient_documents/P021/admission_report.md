## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 26-year-old male collegiate athlete with no significant past medical history who was emergently brought to the Level 1 Trauma Center via advanced life support ambulance after being involved in a catastrophic, high-speed motor vehicle collision where his vehicle rolled over multiple times. The patient was found trapped in the wreckage and required extensive extrication. On initial field assessment, he was found to be comatose with a Glasgow Coma Scale score of 6, displaying abnormal extensor (decerebrate) posturing to painful stimuli, and was immediately intubated in the field for airway protection. Upon arrival in the trauma bay, the patient was profoundly hypotensive with a blood pressure of 82/40 mmHg and highly tachycardic with a heart rate of 144 beats per minute, indicating severe hemorrhagic and neurogenic shock. Physical examination revealed an asymmetric pupillary examination with a blown left pupil, absent gag reflex, and gross, unstable deformities of the left arm and left thigh with absent distal pulses in the left lower extremity. Massive transfusion protocol was instantly activated, and the patient received 4 units of packed red blood cells, 4 units of fresh frozen plasma, and 1 pool of platelets, which successfully stabilized his blood pressure to 110/60 mmHg. An emergent non-contrast CT scan of the head revealed a large, life-threatening left-sided acute subdural hematoma causing an 8mm midline shift and severe compression of the brainstem, alongside early signs of severe bilateral blast injury to the lungs. The patient was taken directly from the scanner to the operating room for an emergent craniectomy and orthopedic limb stabilization.
