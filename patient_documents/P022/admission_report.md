## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 33-year-old female with a mild history of asthma who was admitted to the orthopedic surgical service after presenting to the emergency department following an accidental fall. The patient stated that she was running down a flight of concrete stairs when she missed the final step, causing her right foot to catch and forcibly invert under her full body weight. She experienced an immediate, sharp 'pop' in her right ankle, followed by excruciating pain and an immediate inability to bear any weight on the limb. On physical examination, her right lower extremity was notable for a gross, visible lateral displacement deformity of the foot relative to the tibia. Massive, rapid edema and tracking ecchymosis were present over both the medial and lateral aspects of the ankle joint. The skin was entirely intact, with no tenting or open wounds. Distal neurovascular examination demonstrated strong, bounding dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses, capillary refill time under two seconds, and intact sensation to light touch across all aspects of the foot and toes. An emergent closed reduction was performed in the emergency bay by the orthopedic resident to restore anatomical alignment and protect the soft tissues, and the limb was placed into a well-padded plaster sugar-tong splint. Post-reduction radiographs confirmed persistent widening of the clear space and displacement of the malleoli, requiring inpatient admission for definitive surgical stabilization.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe right ankle pain, immediate inability to bear weight, pronounced localized deformity, massive ecchymosis, and rapid soft tissue swelling over the lateral and medial malleoli.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Closed Displaced Bimalleolar Fracture of the Right Ankle (Weber Type B), Severe Lateral Ligament Disruption

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Admission for lower extremity elevation and ice to reduce soft tissue swelling, followed by definitive operative intervention. The patient underwent an uncomplicated Right Ankle Open Reduction and Internal Fixation (ORIF), utilizing a lateral locking plate with cortical screws for the fibula, and two parallel partially threaded cancellous lag screws for the medial malleolus. Post-operative management focused on multimodal non-morphine pain management, elevation, strict non-weight-bearing status in a posterior splint, and early mobilization with crutches.
