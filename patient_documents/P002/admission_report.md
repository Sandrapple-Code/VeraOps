## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 45-year-old male who was brought to the emergency department via ambulance following a high-speed motorcycle accident wherein he was struck by a turning vehicle, sustaining a direct impact to his right lower extremity. On initial presentation, the patient was alert and oriented to person, place, and time, though he complained of excruciating pain in his right leg and a brief episode of dizziness immediately following the crash. Physical examination revealed a gross deformity of the right mid-shin with a 3-centimeter transverse laceration on the anterior aspect of the lower leg, through which fractured cortical bone fragments were clearly visible. Significant localized swelling, ecchymosis, and soft tissue tenting were observed. Distal neurovascular examination demonstrated palpable, strong dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses, with intact sensation to light touch in all dermatomal distributions of the right foot, and capillary refill under 2 seconds. The right lower limb was immediately splinted in the emergency bay to stabilize the injury and mitigate soft tissue trauma. Intravenous access was established, and the patient was given broad-spectrum antibiotic coverage with IV Clindamycin and Gentamicin due to his severe penicillin allergy, along with a tetanus toxoid booster. Plain radiographs of the right lower extremity confirmed a highly comminuted, displaced mid-shaft fracture of the tibia alongside a distracted fracture of the fibular neck. Due to the open and unstable nature of this Gustilo-Anderson Grade II fracture pattern, the orthopedic surgery service was emergently consulted, and the patient was cleared for immediate operative intervention.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe localized right lower extremity pain, visible bone fragments through a 3cm skin laceration, localized deformity, inability to bear weight, mild transient dizziness.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Open Comminuted Fracture of the Right Tibia and Fibula (Gustilo-Anderson Grade II), Minor Concussion

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Emergent surgical irrigation and debridement, open reduction and internal fixation (ORIF) utilizing an intramedullary nail for the tibia and plate fixation for the fibula. Intravenous non-penicillin prophylactic antibiotics, aggressive multi-modal pain management, and strict non-weight-bearing status.
