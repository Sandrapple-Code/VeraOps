## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 42-year-old male with a significant history of congenital blindness and chronic hypertension who was brought to the emergency department via ambulance after experiencing the sudden onset of left-sided body numbness and coordination failure. The patient's legal partner reported that they were working at their law office when the patient suddenly complained of an intense tingling sensation engulfing the entire left side of his face, left arm, and left leg, followed immediately by an inability to stand straight and severe nausea. Upon arrival in the emergency department within two hours of symptom onset, the patient's vital signs were significant for an elevated blood pressure of 178/96 mmHg, a heart rate of 82 beats per minute, and a normal respiratory rate. Neurological examination was notable for baseline complete blindness with non-reactive pupils, but revealed a new, dense loss of sensation to light touch, pinprick, and temperature across the entire left side of his body, alongside a pronounced left-sided pronator drift and significant dysmetria on left-sided finger-to-nose testing. A code stroke was activated immediately. A non-contrast head CT ruled out hemorrhage, and a subsequent CT angiogram identified an occlusion within the P2 segment of the right posterior cerebral artery. Given the clear timeline and absence of contraindications, the decision was made to proceed with intravenous thrombolytic therapy.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Sudden onset of profound left-sided hemi-sensory loss (face, arm, and leg), acute worsening of ataxia and left-sided dysmetria, mild left facial paresthesia, severe nausea.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Acute Ischemic Stroke of the Right Posterior Cerebral Artery (PCA) Territory, Primary Essential Hypertension, Congenital Blindness

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Because the patient arrived at the emergency department within 2.5 hours of clear symptom onset and met all inclusion criteria, he was given intravenous tissue plasminogen activator (IV tPA / Alteplase) with an infusion dose of 0.9mg/kg. Admission to the stroke unit for strict post-tPA monitoring, including neuro-checks and blood pressure assessments every 15 minutes for the first 2 hours, then hourly. Maintain blood pressure strictly below 180/105 mmHg to prevent hemorrhagic transformation. Initiation of high-dose statin therapy and deferred antiplatelet therapy (Aspirin delayed by 24 hours post-tPA).
