## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 51-year-old male with a highly complex neurological history including a prior thalamic stroke and extensive bilateral hand scarring from a historical motor vehicle accident who was emergently admitted to the neurology step-down service following a presentation for continuous, unremitting focal seizure activity. His spouse, an emergency physician, reported that the patient began experiencing localized, rhythmic twitching of his right fingers which rapidly spread to involve his entire right arm and face, accompanied by an unresponsive staring spell lasting over thirty minutes without intermission. Upon arrival in the emergency bay, the patient was actively experiencing focal motor seizures with altered awareness. His vitals showed a blood pressure of 142/88 mmHg and a heart rate of 98 bpm. Immediate neurological evaluation confirmed active status epilepticus. Large-bore IV access was established, and an emergent status protocol was executed. Given his history of severe adverse reactions to Phenytoin, alternative secondary anti-epileptic agents were utilized. Continuous digital EEG tracking was established at the bedside, which localized a high-burden focal seizure focus. He was admitted to the step-down unit for definitive electrographic tracking and stabilization.
