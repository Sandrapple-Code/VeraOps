## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 38-year-old female with a childhood history of developmental dysplasia of the left hip who presented for admission for a planned, elective left total hip arthroplasty. The patient had been suffering from progressive, severe, unremitting left hip and groin pain for the past five years, which had recently advanced to the point of limiting her activities of daily living, preventing her from exercising, and causing a significant antalgic gait. Comprehensive conservative management, including high-dose non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs, intra-articular corticosteroid injections, and extensive physical therapy, had failed to provide lasting symptomatic relief. Pre-operative clinical evaluation revealed a significant restriction in the range of motion of the left hip, particularly internal rotation and abduction, along with a positive log-roll test. Radiographic evaluation of the pelvis and left hip demonstrated catastrophic joint space obliteration, prominent subchondral sclerosis, and large marginal osteophytes consistent with advanced secondary osteoarthritis. The patient was thoroughly cleared from a cardiovascular and metabolic standpoint for major orthopedic surgery. She was admitted to the orthopedic surgical service on the morning of her scheduled operation with an anticipated routine post-operative course.
