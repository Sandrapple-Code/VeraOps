## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 25-year-old male with an unremarkable past history apart from mild hypertension who was admitted to the orthopedic surgery service via the emergency department following a high-impact mechanism of injury. The patient stated that he was participating in an extreme parkour training session when he fell from a height of ten feet, landing directly onto his flexed left knee against a concrete ledge. He experienced immediate, excruciating pain and an absolute inability to straighten his leg or bear weight. On physical examination, his left knee was grossly deformed, with a 4-centimeter jagged skin laceration directly over the anterior patella through which fractured, displaced bone fragments and a large volume of dark blood were directly visible. He had zero ability to perform an active straight leg raise, confirming a total structural failure of the quadriceps-patellar extensor mechanism. Distal neurovascular checks were completely intact with strong pedal pulses. Due to the open, comminuted nature of this stellate patellar fracture, he was immediately placed in a splint, given broad-spectrum non-cephalosporin antibiotics (IV Vancomycin and Gentamicin), and prepared for emergent surgical reconstruction.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe localized left knee pain, visible fragmentation of the kneecap beneath a 4cm jagged laceration, complete inability to perform an active straight leg raise, joint distension.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Open Comminuted Fracture of the Left Patella (Stellate Type), Left Knee Traumatic Hemarthrosis, Mild Essential Hypertension

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
