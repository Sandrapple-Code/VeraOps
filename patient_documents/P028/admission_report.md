## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 92-year-old male military veteran with a past history of chronic PTSD and essential tremor who was admitted to the orthopedic surgery service after sustaining a mechanical fall at his residence. His brother reported that the patient tripped over an uneven rug, landing directly onto his left hip, and was immediately unable to stand or ambulate. Upon examination in the emergency bay, the patient was alert and highly oriented, though in severe distress from groin pain. His left lower limb was locked in a classic posture of shortening and external rotation, and any passive range of motion of the left hip elicited excruciating pain. Distal neurovascular checks were entirely intact, with palpable dorsalis pedis and posterior tibial pulses and normal sensation. Radiographs of the pelvis confirmed a vertical, highly unstable, displaced fracture of the left femoral neck. Given the intracapsular nature of the fracture and the patient's excellent baseline functional mobility despite his advanced age, the decision was made to admit him for urgent operative management via a bipolar hemiarthroplasty to restore anatomy and avoid the prolonged bedrest-associated complications.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** Severe left groin pain, short-shortened and externally rotated left lower extremity, inability to bear weight or move the left hip, localized bruising.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Displaced Femoral Neck Fracture of the Left Hip (Pauwels Type III), Chronic Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder, Benign Essential Tremor

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Urgent surgical intervention consisting of a left hip hemiarthroplasty to minimize the risk of avascular necrosis of the femoral head and facilitate immediate post-operative mobility. Comprehensive perioperative care focusing on the prevention of post-operative delirium and protection of his stable mental health baseline.
