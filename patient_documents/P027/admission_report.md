## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 44-year-old male with a known background of a congenital bicuspid aortic valve who was admitted to the medicine service after presenting with a two-week history of unremitting, high-grade fevers, rigors, drenching night sweats, and localized left-sided abdominal pain. The patient noted that he had recently returned from extensive travel and initially misidentified his symptoms as a severe viral syndrome. On physical examination in the emergency bay, he was acutely ill, febrile to 39.9 degrees Celsius, tachycardic at 112 beats per minute, and normotensive. Splinter hemorrhages were noted beneath his fingernails, and a detailed cardiac auscultation revealed a prominent, grade 3/6 holosystolic murmur loudest at the right upper sternal border, which was entirely new compared to his last outpatient records. Abdominal palpation revealed guarding and focal tenderness over the left upper quadrant, with mild splenomegaly. Laboratory assessments revealed a severe systemic inflammatory response, significant leukocytosis, and an isolated thrombocytopenia. Given the strong clinical triad of a structural valve abnormality, persistent fevers, a new murmur, and signs of peripheral embolic phenomena, a code endocarditis protocol was initiated. Multiple sets of blood cultures were drawn, and a subsequent transesophageal echocardiogram confirmed a large, mobile aortic valve vegetation, establishing a definitive diagnosis of infective endocarditis.

## Initial Clinical Presentation
- **Chief Complaint:** High spiking fevers up to 40.1 degrees Celsius, shaking chills, drenching night sweats, progressive lethargy, new-onset blowing systolic heart murmur, sharp left upper quadrant abdominal pain.
- **Initial Diagnosis:** Acute Bacterial Endocarditis (Staphylococcus aureus) affecting a Congenital Bicuspid Aortic Valve, Splenic Infarction, Mild Idiopathic Thrombocytopenia

## Admission Orders & Treatment Plan
Intravenous Nafcillin 2g every 4 hours targeted to MSSA endocarditis, serial blood cultures until clearance achieved, strict bed rest, scheduled echocardiographic tracking to monitor vegetation size, consultation with Cardiothoracic Surgery for potential valve replacement if severe insufficiency or heart failure develops.
