## Chief Complaint & Admission History
The patient is a 36-year-old female with an established history of Graves' disease who was admitted to the general medicine service following an emergency room presentation for an acute hypermetabolic crisis. Upon physical examination, she was in a state of extreme psychomotor agitation, hyper-reflexic, flushed, and actively diaphoretic. Her vital signs revealed a severe sinus tachycardia with a heart rate of 136 beats per minute, an elevated blood pressure of 162/94 mmHg, and a low-grade pyrexia of 38.2 degrees Celsius. Marked bilateral exophthalmos (proptosis) and a diffusely enlarged, non-tender thyroid goiter with an audible vascular bruit were easily appreciated on examination. A fine, high-frequency resting tremor was present in both upper extremities. Laboratory evaluations confirmed critical, off-scale elevations in peripheral thyroid hormones with a completely undetectable TSH. Given her high heart rate, fever, and severe neuro-psychiatric agitation, she was managed aggressively to abort a full-blown thyroid storm and admitted for inpatient critical tracking.
